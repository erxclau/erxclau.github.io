About me

Hey y'all! My name is Eric Lau. I'm from Bushwick, Brooklyn and I'm a freshman studying computer science at the University of Michigan. I work at The Michigan Daily where I make data visualizations. I'm also a part of the Michigan Mars Rover team where I (unsurprisingly) program rovers. Outside of those, I'm also exploring topics in computer science like web development and blockchain through extracurriculars or personal projects.

Besides computer science, I have interests in politics, philosophy, and social psychology. I believe programming is ultimately a tool which should be combined with other disciplines and studies. In my free time, I love listening to podcasts and music, and curating Spotify playlists. I also enjoy going on long runs, writing, and thrifting!

Projects
Solon

Solon as an idea has the objective of increasing parent engagement at schools by allowing them to vote on school policies and discuss ideas with faculty. It achieves this goal with a mobile app for parents and an administrative site for staff. The two platforms communicated with each other using an API and database that we built. I worked on a team of several other students as well as with people from Google and the Bushwick Generator. I worked primarily on the mobile app, connecting API routes and implementing user interface designs.

Technologies used: Flutter, Node.js, Express.js, SQL

messenger-data-viz

messenger-data-viz is a tool I built which analyzes Facebook Messenger conversations in terms of raw statistics and natural language. It started out as a single use case to look at the conversation between a friend and I but I then expanded it to be used generally. The analysis is visualuzed in a locally hosted website.

Technologies used: Python, Flask, NLTK, D3.js

Resourceful

Resourceful is a website meant to help people learn about topics they're interested in by providing various resources that are curated by the sites's users. I built it with two friends at SBUHacks 2020. I worked on the layout and styling of the website, and we won Best UI/UX (Aurora Hack).

Technologies used: Node.js, Express.js, MongoDB

Caerus

I attended Stuyvesant High School where one of the school faculty provided a weekly bulletin for opportunities and scholarships in PDF form. Some friends and I thought the bulletin was hard to navigate so we created a website named Caerus to modernize the process of finding and creating items in the bulletin. I worked on implementing the routes, authentication, and an email notification system.

Technologies used: Python, Flask, OAuth2 authentication, SQL, cron jobs

covid3.js

covid3.js is a website that analyzes the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic in non-medical areas like the news, social media, and transportation. My friends and I were heavily inspired by The New York Times and FiveThirtyEight. I worked on analysis of the NYC transportation system as well as a general template for line graphs.

Technologies used: Python, Flask, D3.js

Graphics Engine

I took a course at Stuyvesant where I wrote a graphics engine essentially from scratch. It's capable of realistic lighting, animations, and can take in MDL instructions.

Technologies used: C, Python
